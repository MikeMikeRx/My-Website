Spaceman

Space Program

1

Dive deep into the mathematics that govern cosmic travel. This module covers classical mechanics, Kepler's laws, and advanced orbital maneuvering, teaching cadets how to calculate trajectories and execute precise burns. You'll master the art of trajectory correction and rendezvous operations essential for docking with the International Space Station or future deep-space habitats.

2

Explore the possibility of life beyond Earth and learn to sustain human life in hostile environments. This section focuses on closed-loop life support (CLSS) technologies, recycling oxygen, water, and waste. Cadets will design and maintain bioregenerative systems crucial for long-duration missions to Mars and beyond.

3

Study the future of space travel, moving beyond traditional chemical rockets. We examine high-efficiency methods like ion propulsion, plasma engines, and nuclear thermal rocket concepts. Practical training includes simulation of engine assembly and testing, preparing you to innovate the next generation of interstellar drive systems.

4

Gain hands-on experience with spacewalk procedures and advanced robotics. Training takes place in our Neutral Buoyancy Lab (NBL) where you will practice repairing satellites and constructing large space structures. This course emphasizes emergency protocols, remote manipulation of robotic arms, and complex tool handling in zero-gravity simulation.
